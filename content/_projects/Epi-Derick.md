Epi-Derick is a dystopian simulator game set in a cordoned-off rural town in the UK where an outbreak has rendered every citizen genetically identical to a man named Derick . As the town’s only pharmacist, players must provide care for a town of clones while trying to meet the standards of Vitacure Pharmaceuticals.

The core gameplay is inspired by Papers, Please, centering on reading specific rules to decide what medication to give a customer. Players use the Global Codification of Disorders (GCD) to research ailments, manually cut blister packs to precise dosages with scissors, and organize stock, all while avoiding the fines imposed by Vitacure for any mistakes.

I was the concept creator for the project, aiming for an aesthetic inspired by the works of Mike Klubnika. Throughout development, a lot of features had to be cut due to time and scope considerations. Originally, I planned to introduce more complex illnesses and rules over time, along with multiple endings based on the player's performance. While we didn't reach those specific goals, it was an excellent learning experience in designing a game from beginning to end and onto release, especially when balancing team skill sets against project scope .

My main contributions included building the multiple databases the game uses to generate random customers and a full illness database . Below, you can see examples of the difficulty tables and the complication-to-side-effect tables I designed to determine how mistakes would impact the customers and the resulting fines, or even possible patient death.
